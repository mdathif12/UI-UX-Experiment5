## Algorithm:
Scenario 1: Designing a Health & Fitness Mobile App

1.Identifying and Writing Problem Statements


User pain points:
-Users struggle to stay motivated to exercise regularly.
-Difficulty tracking workouts and progress in one place.
-Confusing interface makes logging meals and exercises tedious.


Problem statements:
-Fitness enthusiasts need a way to easily track and visualize their workout progress because existing apps are complex and time-consuming, which results in decreased motivation and irregular exercise habits.
-Users need a way to receive personalized motivation because generic reminders don't engage them, which leads to dropping out of their fitness routines.

2.Identifying Appropriate Research Methods


Research methods:
-Interviews: To deeply understand motivations and barriers to fitness adherence.
-Diary studies: To capture daily habits and contextual challenges users face with their fitness routines.
-Usability testing: To observe interaction with current app prototypes and identify UI pain points.


Justification:
Interviews provide rich, qualitative insights about personal motivation and challenges. Diary studies help understand real-life contexts over time, and usability testing uncovers usability issues firsthand.

3.Creating Personas


Persona 1: Sarah
-Demographics: 28, Marketing Executive, urban
-Goals: Lose weight, stay consistent with workouts

-Behaviors: Exercises 3x/week, prefers guided workouts
-Pain points: Gets demotivated without clear progress, finds meal logging tedious
-Motivations: Wants easy-to-follow plans and encouragement


Persona 2: Mike
-Demographics: 35, Software Engineer, suburban
-Goals: Build muscle, optimize workout time
-Behaviors: Exercises 5x/week, tracks progress with spreadsheets
-Pain points: Current apps are cluttered, finds it hard to schedule workouts
-Motivations: Wants efficiency and detailed performance data


Scenario 2: Designing an E-commerce Website for Handmade Crafts

1.Identifying and Writing Problem Statements


User pain points:
-Customers find it difficult to discover unique items due to poor categorization.
-Checkout process is complicated, causing abandoned carts.
-Lack of trust because sellers' credibility isn't clear.


Problem statements:
-Online shoppers need a way to quickly find unique handmade products because current navigation is cluttered and confusing, which results in frustration and reduced sales.
-Users need a seamless checkout experience because complicated steps discourage completion, which leads to high cart abandonment rates.

2.Identifying Appropriate Research Methods


Research methods:
-Surveys: To collect broad feedback on user preferences and pain points.
-Usability testing: To test navigation flows and checkout process.

-Card sorting: To improve product categorization and site architecture.


Justification:
Surveys offer quantitative data about customer preferences. Usability testing reveals problems in navigation and checkout usability, while card sorting helps organize product categories in a user-friendly way.

3.Creating Personas


Persona 1: Emily
-Demographics: 24, Graphic Designer, urban
-Goals: Find unique gifts for friends
-Behaviors: Shops online frequently, uses mobile
-Pain points: Frustrated by confusing product categories
-Motivations: Wants quick discovery and inspiration


Persona 2: Raj
-Demographics: 40, Teacher, rural
-Goals: Purchase quality handmade goods for home decoration
-Behaviors: Shops occasionally, prefers desktop
-Pain points: Distrusts sellers due to lack of reviews
-Motivations: Wants secure purchases and seller transparency
